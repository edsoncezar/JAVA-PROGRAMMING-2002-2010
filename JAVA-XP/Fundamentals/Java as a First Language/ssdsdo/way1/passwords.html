Usernames and passwords

This section tells you how to logon to the NT workstations, how  to change your password and how to logout again. It also contains  some advice about how to keep your account secure. Before attempting this part  of the booklet you should have received a account sheetfrom your  software tutor which contains details of your username and  password.

Logging on to a computer system means making a connection from  the computer in one of the labs to a remote computer or more generally to a network. Logging out  means closing that connection and it is essential that you logout every time you  leave the computer you are using so that nobody can use the machine and pretend  to be you. In order to log onto the system it needs to know who you  are, and also to make sure that you are who you say you are.

Your username is how you identify yourself to the remote computer. It is based upon your family name and is up to eight characters long. It is the name by which you will be known to all SBU's computer systems and the name by which you can receive and send electronic mail. Anyone can find out what your username is once they know your personal name.

Your password is how you prove to the system that it is you who is attempting to log on, and not an imposter. It is rather like the four digit code which you may have with your bank card. Anyone may find your card in the street but without the four digit code they cannot get any money out of your account. Likewise anyone may find out what your username is but without your password they will not be able to log on to the system as if they were you.

It is important to make sure that you keep your password secret:

  • choose a password that you will be able to remember.
  • choose a password which is at least six characters long.
  • do not choose anything obvious like the name of your dog or your car registration. Childrens or friends names, football teams or silly words like 'PASSWORD' are the first to be tried
  • you should include a digit {0 ..9} somewhere in the password, but do not include any spaces.
  • do not write the password down.
  • do not tell anyone what your password is.
  • do not allow anyone to look over your shoulder while you are typing in your password.
  • change your password on a regular basis, at least once per term.

If you ever think that someone has found out what your password is, or that someone has been making changes to your account, please contact your software tutor urgently.
